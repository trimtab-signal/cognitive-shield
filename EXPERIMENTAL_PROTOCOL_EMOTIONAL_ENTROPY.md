# Experimental Protocol for the Validation of Algorithmic Emotional Entropy Stripping in the Mitigation of Digital Coercion: A Psychophysiological and Cross-Cultural Analysis

## 1. Introduction: The Neuro-Semiotics of Digital Duress

The rapid assimilation of high-frequency digital communication into the fabric of daily life has precipitated a fundamental shift in the human cognitive environment, a transition often characterized by a phenomenon increasingly recognized in theoretical literature as the "Floating Neutral".1 This concept describes a pervasive loss of baseline epistemic and emotional grounding, wherein the human nervous system, evolved for rich, multi-modal, face-to-face interaction, is forced to process high-velocity text streams stripped of the "ostensive cues"—tone, prosody, and gaze—that historically regulated trust and interpretation.1 In this decoupled state, information does not merely inform; it impacts the receiver with a variability and unpredictability that parallels the thermodynamic concept of entropy. This "Emotional Entropy"—defined here as the stochastic volatility of affective valence within a communication signal—serves as the primary vector for a new form of psychological duress: digital coercion.4

Digital coercion differs mechanistically from its analog counterparts. Where traditional coercion relies on physical presence or immediate material threat, digital coercion exploits the "orienting response" and the brain's "Truth-Default" mechanisms.1 It leverages the "Machine Gun Effect" of rapid-fire notifications and the "Gap Problem" of ambiguous silences to induce a state of hyper-arousal and executive dysfunction.6 This creates a "semantic trauma" where the ambiguity of the signal triggers the amygdala, bypassing the analytical capacities of the prefrontal cortex ("System 2"), and forcing the subject into a state of reactive compliance or maladaptive aggression.7 The result is a degradation of "Coercion Resistance," understood as the capacity to maintain cognitive agency and rational decision-making in the face of external pressure.9

To mitigate this, a novel algorithmic architecture known as the "Cognitive Shield" has been proposed. This system functions as a "prefrontal cortex prosthesis," intervening in the transmission chain to strip "emotional entropy" from incoming signals before they reach the user's perception.1 By utilizing "GenSync" protocols—latency buffering ("The Catcher's Mitt"), sentiment neutralization, and "Bottom Line Up Front" (BLUF) summarization—the Shield aims to "transduce" high-entropy, high-stress inputs into low-entropy, high-clarity payloads.6 While theoretically promising, linking quantum theories of cognition 11 with established psychometric stress models, the efficacy of such "Entropy Stripping" remains empirically unverified.

This report outlines a rigorous experimental protocol to validate the Cognitive Shield. It is designed to measure the suppression of physiological stress responses (HPA axis activation, sympathetic arousal) and the preservation of rational decision-making during simulated coercive interactions. Crucially, recognizing that "stress" is not a monolith, this study integrates a cross-cultural dimension, utilizing the "Spiral Dynamics" framework to map participants into "HumanOS" profiles (Red, Blue, Orange, Green), thereby assessing how deep-seated value systems moderate the reception of algorithmically mediated communication.12 The protocol adheres to the strictest standards of "Informed Consent" and "Psychological Safety," employing an "Iron Dome" data ethics framework to ensure that the study of coercion does not itself become coercive.102. Theoretical Framework2.1 Emotional Entropy as a Psychometric VariableThe concept of entropy, originally derived from thermodynamics to describe the degree of disorder in a system, has found robust application in information theory and, increasingly, in psychology.14 In the context of digital text, "Emotional Entropy" quantifies the uncertainty associated with the affective intent of a message. A message such as "Fine." possesses high emotional entropy because its interpretation relies heavily on missing context; it could signify agreement, passive aggression, or dismissal. This ambiguity forces the brain's predictive coding mechanisms into overdrive, expending metabolic resources ("Cognitive Spoons") to resolve the uncertainty.1Research indicates that high entropy in textual narratives correlates with increased cognitive load and emotional volatility.17 In a coercive context, this volatility is weaponized. The "coercer"—whether a human bad actor or an algorithmic engagement loop—introduces entropy to destabilize the target. "Emotional Entropy Stripping" (EES) is the algorithmic inversion of this process. It applies a "reduction function" to the signal, collapsing the probability distribution of interpretation. For example, the high-entropy input "We need to talk" is translated by the Shield into the low-entropy output: "Sender requests a meeting regarding the project deadline. Tone: Anxious but not hostile".6 This study tests the hypothesis that reducing the informational entropy of the signal directly reduces the physiological entropy (stress) of the receiver.2.2 The Fisher-Escola Synthesis: Quantum Coherence and Decision MakingUnderpinning the "GenSync" architecture is the "Fisher-Escola Synthesis," which posits a link between quantum coherence in biological systems (specifically via Posner molecules and phosphorus nuclear spins) and macroscopic cognitive states.11 In this framework, "System 2" rational thinking—the slow, deliberative processing described by Kahneman—is analogous to a coherent quantum state, fragile and easily collapsed by environmental "noise" (decoherence).Acute stress, particularly the "fight-or-flight" response triggered by digital coercion, acts as a decoherence event.
